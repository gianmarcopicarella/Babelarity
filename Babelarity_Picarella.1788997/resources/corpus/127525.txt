Chantada, is a municipality in the province of Lugo, Galicia, Spain.
Chantada is situated on the left bank of the RÃ­o de Chantada, a small right-hand tributary of the Minho river, and on the main road from Orerse, 29 km S. by W., to Lugo, 45 km N. by E. Chantada is the chief town of the fertile region between the Minho and the heights of El Faro, which mark the western border of the province. Despite the lack of railway communication, it has a thriving trade in grain, flax, hemp, and dairy produce.
