2747 Český Krumlov (Czech: [ˈtʃɛskiː ˈkrʊmlof]) is a main belt asteroid, about 33 kilometers in diameter. It was discovered by Czech astronomer Antonín Mrkos at Kleť Observatory on 19 February 1980. Its provisional designation was 1980 DW.
The asteroid belongs to the Hygiea family and is dark colored (hence probably a carbonaceous C-type).
It is named after the historic Czech town of Český Krumlov, near to the location of the discovering Kleť Observatory.
