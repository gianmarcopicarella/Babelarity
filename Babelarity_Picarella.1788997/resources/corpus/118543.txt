Year 878 (DCCCLXXVIII) was a common year starting on Wednesday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.
Between May 6-12 – Battle of Ethandun (probably either at Edington, Somerset, or Edington, Wiltshire): Alfred the Great of Wessex defeats the Danes of the Danelaw under Guthrum.
May 21 – After a siege of nine months, Syracuse is captured by the Aghlabids of Ifriqiya.
The Treaty of Wedmore divides England between the Anglo-Saxons and the Danes.
Rhodri, the first King of Wales to be known as "the Great", is killed in battle.
Alfonso III of Leon conquers Coimbra from the Umayyads.
The city of Belgrade is first mentioned.
Eochu MacRunn and Girig MacDungal jointly succeed Aed I of Scotland.
The excommunication of the later Pope Formosus is lifted.
Aedh MacRunn, King of the Scots and Picts
Rhodri Mawr, king of Wales
December 31, Emperor Seiwa, Emperor of Japan from 858 to 876. (b. 850)
