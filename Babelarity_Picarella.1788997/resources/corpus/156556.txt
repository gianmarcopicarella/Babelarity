Aichtal (  ) is a town in the district of Esslingen in Baden-Württemberg in southern Germany. It is located 18 km south of Stuttgart.

Aichtal is located on the southern edge of Filderebene the eponymous valley of Aich, a left tributary of the River Neckar, which is separated by the ridge of Galgenberg, Kleinbergle and Schaichberg the Neckar valley. The district Neuhausen, in the corner of Aich and Sheikh located valley formed part, mostly to the Schönbuch nature park. Bulbous marl slopes of the Aichtal valley are covered with meadows and fruit goods. The city covers an altitude of 295 meters at the WWTP Grötzingen to 498 meters on the Betzenberg in Schönbuch.
The city covers an area of 23.64 square kilometers, of which 3.48 square kilometers are inhabited. With 10.49 square kilometers forest areas occupy almost half of the district, including the 8.12-square-kilometer contribution Schönbuch. The expansion of the urban area is 11.0 kilometers from west to east along the Aich and 3.4 km in north-south direction.
Adjacent municipalities are Filderstadt in the north, Wolfschlugen in the northeast, Nürtingen in the east, Neckartailfingen and Schlaitdorf in the south, Walddorfhäslach ( Reutlingen district ) in the southwest and Waldenbuch ( Böblingen district ) in the west.
Aichtal consists of three districts Grötzingen (around 4,300 inhabitants; 7.68 square kilometers), Aich (around 3,100 inhabitants; 6.11 square kilometers) and Neuhausen (about 2,200 inhabitants; 9.85 square kilometers). For Aich heard the spatially separate settlement Rudolfshöhe, the district Grötzingen is the living space attached Bergwirtshaus. Outbound villages are Bombach and Mühlstetten in Aich and Forsthaus, Waldbruderhaus, Grünes Häusle and Brustelberg in Neuenhaus.
On January 1, 1975 at the administrative reform of the city Grötzingen and the independent municipalities Aich and Neuenhaus the new city Grötzingen founded with administrative headquarters in Aich District. After protests from Aich and Neuenhaus, where parts of the population felt by this name downgraded the city on August 1, 1978, renamed in Aichtal.
Grötzinger Crest People living in this area since the Neolithic period. From this period there are finds of stone tools and pottery shards. In a document of Henry IV, Holy Roman Emperor of Worms was mentioned in 1075 for the first time Gretzingan, it was about the vineyards in Grötzingen which states the Kloster Hirsau. The Alemannic settlement arose later than the 7th century and was in today Altgrötzinger Valley. The name probably derives from a clan leader named Gretz. Probably in the first half of the 13th century put one of Grötzinger men in the southern part of town near the small river Aich a moated castle with a farmyard. The foundation of the city took place in 1275 by Grötzingen knight Diepold of Bernshausen, King Rudolf of Habsburg supported and established the city as a fortification against the winning influence of Württembergian Nürtingen. 1304 Grötzingen is first referred to in documents as a town, the inhabitants of former village moved into its walls, the former village fell. In 1337 sold Diepolds son the town of Grötzingen to Württemberg. From the 14th to the 16th century was Grötzingen seat of office, among them were Aich, Neckartailfingen, Neckartenzlingen, Neuhausen and Wolfschlugen. In the Schmalkaldic War, the citizens of Grötzingen had in 1546 sold out their guns because they had not enough money. When approached marauding soldiers, they dug out wooden fountain pipes and pushed it into the loopholes of the ramparts. Because the approaching soldiers held them for guns, they proceeded without attacking the city. In the years 1634/1635 were 243 and 194 Grötzinger Neckartailfinger citizens who took refuge here after the destruction of their village refuge, the plague victim. After the Thirty Years' War the city counted only one third of the population. Grötzingen was the second richest to poorest place the Office Nürtingen. The emergency has forced many poor inhabitants in the 18th century to emigrate, mostly to North America. A major fire destroyed in 1845 in the village 13 buildings, including the town hall and the schoolhouse.
