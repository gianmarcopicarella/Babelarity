Jeon Ji-yoon (born October 15, 1990) is a South Korean singer, rapper and actress. She was previously a member of South Korean girl group 4Minute and its sub-group 2Yoon.
Jeon Ji-yoon was born on October 15, 1990 in Suwon, South Korea. She graduated from Byeongjeom High School and is currently attending Kyung Hee University, majoring in Post Modern Music.
Jiyoon was chosen as a member of 4Minute in 2009. The 5-member girl group official debuted on June 18, 2009 with performing their debut single "Hot Issues" on M Countdown.
On October 15, 2009, she was a featured artist in Woo Yi Kyung's album Look At Me with the song "Look At Me". On April 13, 2010, she was a featured artist in Lee Hyori's fourth album H-Logic with the song "Bring It Back" together with After School's Bekah. She sang in the soundtrack of MBC's My Princess, with the song "Oasis", released on February 7, 2011. She was also featured in Hyuna's album Bubble Pop! in the song "Downtown". The album was released on July 5, 2011.
Ji-yoon joined KBS's Immortal Songs 2 in June 2011 as a replacement of Song Ji Eun of Secret due to their Japanese promotions. "I felt that her vocals were more charming than [4Minute's] main vocalist Gayoon," the producer of the show said. On the show, professionals sing competitively and are judged by a studio audience of 200. She sang "We Meet Again" in an episode broadcast on July 15. And joining on KBS's Immortal Songs 2, she won together with DJ Koo with the song "I (Nan)" originally by Clon against Super Junior's Kyu Hyun and Son Ho Young.
Gayoon and her bandmates Jiyoon was formed subgroup called 2Yoon in 2013. 2Yoon released their debut EP "Harvest Moon" with lead single "24/7" on 17 January 2013.The sub-group official debuted on January 17 on M Countdown.
On September 2014 was revealed that Ji-yoon will making her actress debut with the online drama Dreaming CEO.
