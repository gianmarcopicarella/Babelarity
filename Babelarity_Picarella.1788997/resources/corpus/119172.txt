Year 1109 (MCIX) was a common year starting on Friday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.
The Almoravid Ali ibn Yusuf organizes an auto-da-fé of the works of Al-Ghazali in front of the great mosque of Cordoba.
The Crusaders capture Tripoli and Beirut.
Fulk V becomes Count of Anjou.
August 10 – The Poles, led by Bolesław III Wrymouth, defeat the Pomeranians in the Battle of Nakło.
August 24 – The Poles defeat the Holy Roman Empire in the Battle of Głogów.
August 24 – The Poles again defeat the Holy Roman Empire in the Battle of Hundsfeld.
Alfonso I of Aragon marries Urraca of Castile.
The Almoravid army led by Ali ibn Yusuf fails to reconquer Toledo (lost in 1085).
Anselm of Laon becomes chancellor of Laon.
July 25 – King Afonso I of Portugal (d. 1185; birthdate disputed)
April 21 – Anselm of Canterbury, Archbishop of Canterbury
April 28 – Abbot Hugh of Cluny (b. 1024)
July 1 – King Alfonso VI of Castile (b. 1040)
William-Jordan, Count of Cerdagne and Tripoli
